Surveys



As for the product analysis, we surveyed fifty people. All ranging from family members to friends. We explained our invention and had them give their inputs. Overall, they all agreed that this is a useful and feasible idea. They liked how it can make renewable energy and can be brought anywhere and can be used anywhere. We also told them that this product was really effective, as it makes 45 watts an hour. This tower over all solar portable chargers.

However, there were also some complaints. They critiqued the build quality, as it did not look very appealing and seemed like a lot to put on a bike. They also questioned the price of the product as well. Finally, they questioned how this would be assembled. As for the build quality, we could not think of a way to cover it. Anything that would cover it would interfere with wheels or the biker's leg. It is hard to hide the chain and motor without it being an interference. The price took a little while to calculate, but after finding the materials online, we estimated the price to be around $100 to $150. This seems expensive, but after looking at other alternatives for other chargers as good as ours, the $150 pales in comparison to their prices. Finally, the assembly. This would definitely take a while to assemble. The motor and battery would take a while to attach. However, the chain would be easy to connect.
